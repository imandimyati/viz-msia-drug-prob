Malaysia is among the country that carry the heaviest sentence when it comes to drug abuse. Under the Dangerous Drug Act 1952 Section 39(B) – trafficking in dangerous drug – offenders shall be punished on conviction with death.

This section of the act clearly shows how Malaysia is strongly against the trafficking of dangerous drug, offer to traffic in a dangerous drug or even do or offer to do an act preparatory to or for the purpose of trafficking in a dangerous drug.

Under Section 39(A)(2) every person found guilty of an offence against this act where the offence is not punishable with death shall instead be punished with imprisonment for life or for a term which shall not be less than five years. Also, under the act the offender shall be punished with whipping of not less than ten strokes.

This article explores how many people had been arrested and what crimes have been committed.

Police Arrest Based on Urine Test: Numbers are still high

Malaysia Royal Police Force have two operation that focuses on arresting people suspected being a drug addict – Ops Berkas and Ops Tapis. These operations usually involve urine test to check whether it is positive for drug.

However, these arrests do not automatically mean they’ll be charged under the Dangerous Drug Act 1952. These arrests are just at the initial level and further investigation is needed. Arrested and Remanded: Huge gap between men and women

Malaysia’s prison department collects the data on its institutions. Under the department, it oversees prisons, moral rehabilitation centers and Henry Gurney schools. The residents for prisons usually range from different kind of crimes, unlike Henry Gurney schools and moral rehabilitation centers.

Henry Gurney schools are allocated for juvenile cases. In other words, those who are underage when they commit a crime are placed here. For moral rehabilitation center, it is used to place persons who have committed crime under the Dangerous Drugs Act (Special Preventive Measures) Act 1985. According to this act, whether the detainee has been or is associated with any activity relating to or involving the trafficking in dangerous drugs, they should be retained in the interest of public order.

According to the prison department, in 2014 the number of drug convicts in prison institutions is 13,494 people (including remand prisoners/unconvicted). This amount represents 26.99% of the total number of prisoners in the prison in Malaysia (49,989 people) in 2014.

Drug Crimes Committed: Despite harsh punishment, drug crimes still on the rise

As explained before, Malaysia carries among the harshest punishment for drug abuse. Under the Dangerous Drug Act 1952 Section 39(B) – trafficking in dangerous drug – offenders shall be punished on conviction with death. As can be seen from the chart below, the number of people committing drug trafficking crime is on the rise – even almost reaching 5,000 in 2014.

While the punishment is death, executions may not necessarily be carried out. According to Amnesty International, despite retaining the death sentence in its law, Malaysia had condemned around 38 people on death sentences in 2014. In the same year, only around two people were executed.

Another section with a harsh punishment of life sentence is Section 39(A), even those who commit this crime is on the rise. It seems that even with the prescribed harsh punishments, it does not deter people from committing the crime.
